# One pinned poll discipline with progress-based stall detection, and a timed two-chunk download preflight

The first live smoke of the Extractor cutover (2026-07-22, against Kntnt Extractor 0.2.0) surfaced two client-side fragilities that the upstream faults merely exposed. First, the status poll aborted on a single stall: `GET /extractions/{id}` can legitimately block for tens of seconds ([kntnt-extractor#19](https://github.com/Kntnt/kntnt-extractor/issues/19)), and a host whose continuation loopback is dead advances a job only at cron-backstop cadence over many minutes ([kntnt-extractor#18](https://github.com/Kntnt/kntnt-extractor/issues/18)) — the bootstrap poll got two zero-byte responses on 20 s / 60 s timeouts and reported `FAILED`, even though the job completed fine and was downloadable minutes later. Second, the health-check download preflight submitted a single tiny structure-only table: a one-chunk job completes on the create-time kick alone, so the preflight passed on a host whose *continuation* loopback was dead, and the fault only surfaced mid-run, on the heavy extraction, as an apparent hang. Upstream, both faults were fixed in kntnt-extractor 0.2.1 (2026-07-23, its ADR-0010): time-budgeted ticks, continuation moved after the response with detach-then-in-process-tick, a hardened nudge — with no REST contract change. But the client cannot tell a fixed host from an unfixed one: `GET /status` deliberately carries only `api_version` (unchanged at 2), and `/environment`'s `active_plugins` is a bare list of plugin-file paths with no versions, by deliberate least-disclosure. So both fixes here must be purely behavioural — hardening that works identically against a fixed (≥ 0.2.1) and an unfixed (≤ 0.2.0) Extractor, costing nothing on fixed hosts.

**Decision 1 — one canonical, numerically pinned poll discipline for all three poll loops** (the preflight, the bootstrap, and the main extraction): poll every 15 s after a successful poll with a 120 s per-request timeout; on a transport timeout, connection error, or 5xx, log and retry after 30 s (60 s from the second consecutive failure), resetting on the next success; report the `progress` counters between polls; and return failure *only* on `state == "failed"`, a vanished job (`404`), no progress-advance within a 10-minute stall window, or exhaustion of the loop's overall wall-clock budget — 10 minutes for the preflight, 15 for the bootstrap, 60 (`poll_max_wait_seconds = 3600`) for the main extraction. "Advance" means a state change or an increase in `progress.tables_done + progress.files_done`; a `queued` job carries no counters, so its stall clock runs on state alone. The 15 s cadence is not arbitrary: it matches the Extractor's default `tick_budget`, and after kntnt-extractor#19's fix every poll on a detachable host advances the job in-process by up to one budget after the response is sent — polling is propulsion, not just observation, so a steady cadence drives the job and is never backed off while polls succeed. Every poll is also WordPress traffic, which is what fires WP-Cron's watchdog backstop on an otherwise quiet site — a second reason steady beats sparse on unfixed hosts. The 10-minute stall window spans several cron-backstop cycles, so a slow-but-advancing job is never mistaken for a wedged one.

**Decision 2 — the download preflight becomes a timed two-chunk probe with an operator gate on a slow verdict.** The probe is exactly two structure-only tables and no files — `{table_prefix}options` and `{table_prefix}users`, both present in every WordPress install, DDL-only so size is irrelevant. Two tables mean exactly two packaging chunks, and on an unfixed host the second chunk only lands through the continuation path — the exact path a one-chunk job never exercises. The verdict is the measured wall time from the `201` to the first `ready` poll: ≤ 90 s passes silently (a fixed detachable host does both chunks at create time; a healthy loopback does them in one or two quick ticks); slower but ready within the 10-minute preflight budget passes with a loud warning — the host advances jobs at backstop cadence, so the heavy extraction may be impractically slow; upgrade to ≥ 0.2.1, or if already there, fix the host's loopback, because a host that can neither detach nor reach its loopback advances one 15 s packaging budget per cron cycle and steady polling cannot drive it — and an operator gate on whether to continue (a `--yes` run, with no operator present to own that choice, aborts with the warning as the remediation); not ready within the budget aborts the health check with that same message.

**Rejected alternatives.** *Consuming a `loopback_ok` status field*: proposed upstream, explicitly dropped in kntnt-extractor#18 ("fix 5") because loopback health no longer gates throughput after the fix — the signal will never exist, and nothing here may depend on it. *Gating on the plugin release version*: `GET /status` carries only `api_version` by design (the `Status_Controller`'s deliberate least-disclosure), 0.2.1 kept it at 2, and no side channel exists — version gating is impossible, which is precisely why the hardening is behavioural. *Aborting on the first timeout*: the original fault — it conflates a transport hiccup with a terminal state on a surface where a blocking poll is documented, legitimate behaviour.

## Consequences

- The poll discipline is stated in full, with the same pinned literals, in the three surfaces that own a poll loop's execution — `skills/clone/SKILL.md` §5, `skills/pull/SKILL.md` §5, and `agents/extract-transfer.md` — and by compact reference (with the 15-minute budget) in `agents/discovery-classify.md`; the preflight's inline loop in both skills' health-check step 4 carries it too. `tests/test_poll_discipline_consistency.py` binds the literals; `tests/test_preflight_probe_consistency.py` binds the two-table probe and the retirement of the one-table wording.
- `extract-transfer`'s evidence block gains the poll telemetry — total poll wall time, transport failures retried, final `progress` counters — so the orchestrator can see how a run behaved, not just that it finished.
- The `429` hard stop on `POST /extractions` is untouched: a still-active job is a sequencing fault, not a transport fault, and is never retried.
- The health check's sweep-before-preflight ordering and its never-concurrent rule (issue #15) are untouched; the probe changes shape, not position.
- The per-job poll's `progress` shape — `{ id, state, download_url, progress?, error? }` with `progress: { tables_done, tables_total, files_done, files_total }` while running or ready — is now documented in the spec's control-channel section, since the stall rule depends on it.
- Nothing here distinguishes Extractor versions at run time: the discipline and the probe behave identically against ≤ 0.2.0 and ≥ 0.2.1 hosts, and the API-version floor stays ≥ 2, unchanged.
